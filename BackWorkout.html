Back Day WorkOuts

A back day workout tries to concentrate on movements that bolster and develop back muscle. You definitely break up your exercises into body component splits if you decide to undertake a back day.

Simply said, this is a method of scheduling your exercises. It simply implies that you must concentrate just on one or two particular body areas during each exercise, like your back, as part of a bodybuilding plan with a lot of volume (sets and repetitions).

It is advisable to carry out the quantity of sets and repetitions required to promote strength and/or muscular development.

    1. Lat Pull Down

  • 4 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Use a lat pulldown machine or an overhand grip on a pull-up bar if you can do bodyweight pull-ups.

Your lats, which are sizable muscles on the sides of your back, are worked during a lat pulldown. Place your legs firmly behind the cushioned brace while you sit down at a lat pulldown machine. Choose the weight that you want to utilise. Grasp the bar with an overhand grip that feels natural to you. Resistance bands that are fastened to a fixed place may also be used for this activity.

With your back slightly arched and your chest up, brace your core. Lean back a little bit.

With your elbows pulled back, bring the handle down to the top of your chest.

As soon as the bar reaches your chest, slowly raise it back up. Keep the weight stack from touching.

    2. Dumbbell Deadlift

  • 4 sets of 8-10 reps
  • Use a lat pulldown machine or an overhand grip on a pull-up bar if you can do bodyweight pull-ups.

Deadlifts are a great lower back workout that work a variety of muscles, including your whole core, glutes, and hip flexors.

Standing facing the dumbbells with your feet shoulder-width apart, place two dumbbells on the ground.

After pushing your bottom behind you, hinge your hips and bend your knees. Maintain a straight back. With your arms fully extended, reach for the dumbbells and take an overhand hold of them.

Dumbbells should be held near to your shins as you stand up holding them with palms facing your body. Do not round your back as you extend your hips forward.

  • 4 sets of 8-10 reps

    4. Dumbbell Pullover

  • 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Lie on a bench and hold a dumbbell with both hands to perform pullovers.

Pullovers may be back- or chest-focused, focusing mostly on the lat muscles. You may focus on the back for this exercise with only a little adjustment to your technique

Lay down on a bench with your knees stacked beneath your feet and your upper back perpendicular to the bench. Maintain a straight posture by flexing your hips; do not let your hips drop. Dumbbells should be held between the hands with the bell end in the palms facing up.

With your elbows slightly bent, raise the dumbbell above your chest.

Maintain a tiny bend in your elbows as you turn them in towards your body, keeping your shoulder blades down and back.

    5. Inverted Row

  • 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Use a Smith machine or a TRX suspension trainer to perform inverted rows.

Your upper and lower back as well as your traps will be worked out with inverted rows, a beginner-friendly alternative to a barbell row. Legs may be bent (easier) or extended (harder) to change the difficulty of inverted rows. The lower the bar is positioned, the harder they are.

Lay on your back under a barbell that has been raised to waist height in a rack. Reach up and take an overhand grip that is broader than shoulder width on the bar.

With your back straight and your abs tight, lift your chest to the bar. Retract your elbows. Squeeze your back muscles for a count of one, then gently return to the beginning position

    6. One Arm Dumbbell Row

  • 3 sets of 10-12 reps
  • Place one knee and one hand on a bench for support.

Your upper and lower back as well as your lower traps will be worked by the unilateral exercise known as the one-arm dumbbell row.

Place a dumbbell on the floor on each side of your right knee as you stand at the end of a bench. Your back may be supported by a bench that is flat or inclined. Your right hand should be placed on the bench for support as you stoop until your body is parallel to it.

With your left hand in an overhand hold and your palm facing you, reach down to pick up a dumbbell.

Maintain a straight back and a tight core.

Squeeze your back muscles, inhale, and then slowly return to the beginning position.
