HIIT Day WorkOuts

A back day workout tries to concentrate on movements that bolster and develop back muscle. You break up your exercises into body component splits if you decide to undertake a back day.

Simply said, this is a method of scheduling your exercises. It simply implies that you must concentrate just on one or two body areas during each exercise, like your back, as part of a bodybuilding plan with a lot of volume (sets and repetitions).

It is advisable to carry out the quantity of sets and repetitions required to promote strength and/or muscular development.

    1. Grapevine With a Jump

  • Start by stepping to the right, crossing your left foot behind your right, then step to the right again, crossing your left foot in front.

Start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and your arms at your sides. Step with your left foot to the left, then step with your right foot behind your left. Step your left foot out to the left once more after that.

The grapevine says this. After that, leap up and clap while landing with your legs slightly bent. Replicate, this time going to the right. Five times on each side, alternate between the left and the right.

×

    2. Burpees

  • Start by standing, then drop into a squat position and place your hands on the ground.
  • Kick your feet back into a plank position.

Start by squatting down and placing your feet shoulder-width apart. Put your weight on your hands by placing both on the ground. Push yourself up into the push up position by kicking your feet back behind you.

Make sure your back is straight and your core is engaged while you do one push up. Once you are back in a squat stance, spring your feet forward and stand up. Jump up and raise your arms over your head. Straighten out into a squat stance after landing, then repeat. Complete a total of 10 burpees.

    3. Skaters

  • Start by jumping to the right, extending your left leg behind you and crossing it behind your right leg.
  • Jump to the left, reversing the motion.

Start with your arms at your sides and your legs slightly wider than shoulder width apart. Step the left leg at a small angle behind the right leg to do a reverse lunge. A 90-degree bend will be made in the right knee.

Push off the right foot, leap to the left, land with the left foot forward and the right leg behind in a reverse lunge stance, and then exchange sides. As you leap, swing your arms. Channel your inner speed skater by alternating your arms as you flip sides! For a total of 20 seconds, repeat 5 times on each side.

    4. Mountain Climbers

  • Start in a push-up position.
  • Bring one knee toward your chest, then quickly switch to the other knee.

Start in a plank posture with a straight back, low hips, and a tight core. Tap your right foot on the ground as you tuck your right knee beneath your chest and drive it towards your left elbow.

As you swiftly bring your left knee to your right elbow and tap your foot on the floor, step back to plank. Maintain a steady speed while you keep switching your legs. Remind yourself to breathe, and focus on tightening your hamstrings, glutes, and abdominals. 20 overall, with 10 on each side.

    5. Plank Ups

  • Start in a plank position on your forearms.
  • Push up onto your hands, one hand at a time, then lower back onto your forearms.

Beginning with a plank on your forearms. To get into a high plank posture, press your right hand into the ground first, followed by your left hand.

Make sure the wrists are aligned with the shoulders. Then, squat down onto your right forearm before lowering back to a plank position with your left forearm. Lead five times with your right foot, then five times with your left.
